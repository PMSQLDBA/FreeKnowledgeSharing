**2026 US and India job markets**:

Here are the key shifts:

* Traditional DBA-only roles continue to decline.
* Employers increasingly seek hybrid profiles: **Database + Cloud + Automation + AI**.
* Infrastructure as Code, CI/CD, observability, and scripting are now expected, not optional.
* AI-assisted development and operations have become part of everyday workflows.
* Data platforms (Snowflake, Databricks, Microsoft Fabric) are growing faster than traditional on-prem data warehouses.
* Kubernetes, Docker, Linux, Python, and Git are commonly listed even for DBA positions.

## Current Market Reality

For both the US and India, the strongest candidate profile is no longer "DBA" in isolation. Hiring managers increasingly look for professionals who combine database expertise with cloud, automation, and engineering skills.

A competitive profile in today's market typically includes:

* Strong SQL and database administration fundamentals across multiple platforms.
* Proficiency in **Azure** and **AWS**, with exposure to managed database services.
* **Python** plus at least one shell scripting language (PowerShell or Bash).
* **Git**, **GitHub**, CI/CD, and **Terraform** as core engineering skills.
* **Linux**, **Docker**, and **Kubernetes** experience.
* Familiarity with modern data platforms such as **Databricks**, **Snowflake**, and **Microsoft Fabric**.
* Working knowledge of **AI-assisted development** and automation.
* Strong understanding of observability, security, and operational reliability.

One caution: don't try to become an expert in every technology on this list. The job market rewards **depth plus adjacent breadth**, not superficial familiarity with dozens of tools. A solid combination of database engineering, one or two cloud platforms, automation, and modern data tooling is generally more valuable than a long list of beginner-level skills across every ecosystem.
